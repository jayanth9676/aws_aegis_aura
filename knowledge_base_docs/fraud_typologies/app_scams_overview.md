# Authorized Push Payment (APP) Scam Overview

## What is APP Fraud?

Authorized Push Payment (APP) fraud is a type of financial fraud where victims are manipulated into willingly authorizing payments from their own accounts to accounts controlled by criminals. Unlike unauthorized fraud where credentials are stolen, APP scams exploit psychological manipulation and social engineering.

## Key Characteristics

- **Authorized by victim**: The customer themselves initiates and authorizes the payment
- **Real-time payments**: Typically use instant payment rails (Faster Payments, etc.)
- **Irreversible**: Once authorized, funds transfer immediately
- **Social engineering**: Relies on psychological manipulation, not technical exploits

## Common APP Scam Types

### 1. Impersonation Scams
- **Bank impersonation**: Fraudster poses as bank's fraud department
- **Police impersonation**: Claims of criminal investigation
- **Government impersonation**: Tax authority, regulatory body
- **Utility company**: Threatening service disconnection

### 2. Invoice/Payment Redirection Scams
- Compromised business email accounts
- Fraudulent invoices with changed bank details
- Supply chain payment intercept

### 3. Romance Scams
- Long-term relationship building
- Emotional manipulation
- Fabricated emergencies requiring urgent payment
- Promises of future meeting/relationship

### 4. Investment Scams
- Fake investment opportunities
- Cryptocurrency schemes
- High-return promises
- Pressure to invest quickly

### 5. Purchase Scams
- Fake online marketplaces
- Non-existent goods or services
- Advance fee fraud

## Red Flags and Indicators

### Urgency and Pressure
- "Act now or lose money/account"
- "Don't hang up"
- "Don't tell anyone about this"

### New Payee + High Amount
- First-time payment to recipient
- Amount significantly higher than typical transactions
- Round numbers (£1000, £5000, etc.)

### Customer Under Duress
- Active phone call during transaction
- Hesitant typing patterns
- Copy-pasting account details
- Multiple authentication failures

### Verification Mismatches
- Confirmation of Payee (CoP) mismatch
- Name doesn't match account

### Behavioral Anomalies
- Unusual time of day
- Device not recognized
- Location inconsistencies
- Session duration anomalies

## Prevention Strategies

1. **Effective Friction**: Dynamic warnings at point of payment
2. **Verification**: Strong Confirmation of Payee checks
3. **Customer Education**: Clear scam awareness messaging
4. **Delayed Settlement**: Time delays for high-risk payments
5. **Analyst Escalation**: Human review of suspicious transactions

## Response Procedures

When APP scam suspected:
1. Block/hold payment immediately
2. Contact customer on verified number
3. Verify transaction legitimacy independently
4. Document all interactions
5. File SAR if fraud confirmed
6. Attempt fund recovery through receiving bank

## Regulatory Context

**UK PSR Mandatory Reimbursement** (effective Oct 7, 2024):
- PSPs must reimburse APP scam victims
- Maximum claim: £415,000
- Liability shared between sending and receiving banks
- Exceptions for gross negligence by customer

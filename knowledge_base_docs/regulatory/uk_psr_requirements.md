# UK Payment Services Regulations (PSR) Requirements for APP Fraud

## Overview
The UK Payment Services Regulations (PSR) mandate specific requirements for Authorized Push Payment (APP) fraud prevention and reimbursement, effective from October 7, 2024.

## Key Requirements

### 1. Mandatory Reimbursement
- **Scope**: All PSPs (Payment Service Providers) must reimburse APP scam victims
- **Maximum Claim**: £415,000 per victim
- **Timeframe**: Reimbursement within 5 business days of fraud confirmation
- **Shared Liability**: Liability shared between sending and receiving banks

### 2. Exceptions for Gross Negligence
Reimbursement can be denied if customer demonstrates gross negligence:
- Ignoring clear warnings about potential fraud
- Failing to verify payee details when prompted
- Continuing transaction after being warned of scam indicators
- Sharing authentication credentials with third parties

### 3. Prevention Requirements

#### Confirmation of Payee (CoP)
- **Mandatory**: All PSPs must implement CoP for account name verification
- **Real-time**: Name matching must occur before payment authorization
- **Clear Messaging**: Mismatches must be clearly communicated to customers
- **Fallback**: Partial matches require additional verification

#### Customer Education
- **Scam Awareness**: Regular education about APP fraud typologies
- **Warning Systems**: Dynamic warnings at point of payment
- **Red Flag Education**: Clear communication of scam indicators
- **Reporting Channels**: Easy access to fraud reporting mechanisms

### 4. Data Sharing Requirements

#### Industry Intelligence
- **Threat Intelligence**: Sharing of known scam patterns and indicators
- **Mule Account Data**: Cross-bank sharing of identified mule accounts
- **Fraud Typologies**: Regular updates on emerging scam types
- **Best Practices**: Sharing of effective prevention strategies

#### Regulatory Reporting
- **SAR Filing**: Suspicious Activity Reports within 24 hours
- **PSR Reporting**: Quarterly reports on APP fraud statistics
- **Compliance Monitoring**: Regular audits of prevention measures
- **Performance Metrics**: Tracking of prevention effectiveness

### 5. Technical Requirements

#### Real-time Monitoring
- **Transaction Screening**: Real-time analysis of payment patterns
- **Risk Scoring**: Dynamic risk assessment for each transaction
- **Velocity Checks**: Monitoring of unusual payment patterns
- **Device Fingerprinting**: Analysis of device and location anomalies

#### Customer Communication
- **Multi-channel**: SMS, email, in-app notifications
- **Clear Language**: Plain English warnings and explanations
- **Urgency Indicators**: Clear communication of time-sensitive risks
- **Action Guidance**: Specific steps for customers to take

### 6. Compliance Monitoring

#### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **Prevention Rate**: Percentage of attempted scams prevented
- **False Positive Rate**: Percentage of legitimate transactions blocked
- **Response Time**: Time from detection to customer contact
- **Reimbursement Time**: Time from fraud confirmation to reimbursement

#### Audit Requirements
- **Annual Reviews**: Comprehensive assessment of prevention measures
- **Penetration Testing**: Regular testing of fraud prevention systems
- **Staff Training**: Regular training on fraud detection and prevention
- **System Updates**: Regular updates to fraud detection algorithms

### 7. Cross-border Considerations

#### International Payments
- **Enhanced Due Diligence**: Additional checks for cross-border payments
- **Country Risk Assessment**: Risk scoring based on destination country
- **Currency Controls**: Monitoring of unusual currency patterns
- **Sanctions Screening**: Real-time screening against sanctions lists

#### Regulatory Cooperation
- **International Standards**: Alignment with global fraud prevention standards
- **Information Sharing**: Cross-border sharing of fraud intelligence
- **Joint Investigations**: Cooperation with international law enforcement
- **Best Practice Exchange**: Regular exchange of prevention strategies

### 8. Customer Rights and Protections

#### Right to Information
- **Clear Terms**: Transparent communication of fraud prevention measures
- **Risk Disclosure**: Clear explanation of customer responsibilities
- **Appeal Process**: Right to appeal reimbursement decisions
- **Independent Review**: Access to independent dispute resolution

#### Data Protection
- **GDPR Compliance**: Full compliance with data protection regulations
- **Data Minimization**: Collection of only necessary data for fraud prevention
- **Consent Management**: Clear consent for data processing
- **Right to Erasure**: Customer right to data deletion where appropriate

### 9. Implementation Timeline

#### Phase 1 (Immediate)
- Implement basic CoP functionality
- Deploy customer education materials
- Establish fraud reporting channels
- Begin data sharing with industry partners

#### Phase 2 (3 months)
- Deploy advanced risk scoring systems
- Implement real-time monitoring
- Establish customer communication systems
- Begin regulatory reporting

#### Phase 3 (6 months)
- Full implementation of all requirements
- Complete staff training programs
- Establish audit and monitoring systems
- Achieve full compliance with all PSR requirements

### 10. Penalties and Enforcement

#### Regulatory Sanctions
- **Financial Penalties**: Up to £50,000 per violation
- **Business Restrictions**: Limitations on payment services
- **Public Disclosure**: Publication of non-compliance
- **Corrective Actions**: Mandatory implementation of improvements

#### Customer Compensation
- **Direct Compensation**: Reimbursement of fraud losses
- **Additional Damages**: Compensation for distress and inconvenience
- **Legal Costs**: Reimbursement of customer legal expenses
- **Interest Payments**: Interest on delayed reimbursements

This regulatory framework ensures comprehensive protection for customers while maintaining the integrity of the payment system and promoting innovation in fraud prevention.

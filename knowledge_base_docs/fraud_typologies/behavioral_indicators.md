# Behavioral Indicators and Risk Assessment

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive coverage of behavioral indicators that can be used to detect potential fraud, particularly in the context of APP scams and social engineering attacks.

## Customer Behavior Indicators

### 1. Communication Patterns

#### Verbal Indicators
- **Hesitation**: Unusual pauses or hesitations in speech
- **Coaching**: Evidence of someone else providing answers
- **Scripted Responses**: Overly rehearsed or scripted answers
- **Inconsistency**: Contradictory information or changing stories
- **Urgency**: Excessive urgency or pressure to complete transactions quickly

#### Written Communication
- **Language Complexity**: Sudden changes in language complexity or style
- **Typing Patterns**: Unusual typing speed, errors, or corrections
- **Copy-Paste Behavior**: Evidence of copying information from external sources
- **Response Time**: Unusual delays in responses to prompts or questions
- **Formatting**: Unusual formatting or structure in communications

### 2. Transaction Behavior

#### Timing Patterns
- **Unusual Hours**: Transactions at unusual times of day or night
- **Rush Transactions**: Attempts to complete transactions very quickly
- **Multiple Attempts**: Repeated attempts to complete the same transaction
- **Session Duration**: Unusually long or short session times
- **Time Pressure**: Evidence of external time pressure

#### Amount Patterns
- **Round Numbers**: Preference for round numbers (e.g., £1000, £5000)
- **Unusual Amounts**: Amounts significantly different from typical transactions
- **Full Balance**: Attempts to transfer entire account balance
- **Incremental Increases**: Gradual increases in transaction amounts
- **Currency Preferences**: Unusual currency preferences or conversions

#### Payee Patterns
- **New Payees**: First-time payments to new recipients
- **Unverified Payees**: Payments to unverified or suspicious recipients
- **Multiple Payees**: Payments to multiple recipients in short timeframes
- **International Transfers**: Unusual international payment patterns
- **Cryptocurrency**: Payments to cryptocurrency exchanges or wallets

### 3. Device and Location Behavior

#### Device Patterns
- **New Devices**: Login attempts from new or unrecognized devices
- **Device Switching**: Frequent switching between different devices
- **Browser Changes**: Sudden changes in browser or browser settings
- **VPN Usage**: Use of VPNs or proxy servers
- **Mobile vs Desktop**: Unusual switching between mobile and desktop platforms

#### Location Patterns
- **Geographic Anomalies**: Login attempts from unusual geographic locations
- **Travel Patterns**: Unusual travel patterns or rapid location changes
- **IP Address Changes**: Frequent changes in IP addresses
- **Network Anomalies**: Unusual network characteristics or configurations
- **Time Zone Mismatches**: Mismatches between claimed location and time zone

### 4. Authentication Behavior

#### Login Patterns
- **Failed Attempts**: Multiple failed login attempts
- **Password Changes**: Frequent password changes or resets
- **Security Questions**: Difficulty answering security questions
- **Multi-Factor Authentication**: Issues with MFA or 2FA
- **Session Management**: Unusual session management behavior

#### Verification Behavior
- **Document Upload**: Issues with document upload or verification
- **Identity Verification**: Problems with identity verification processes
- **Address Verification**: Issues with address verification
- **Phone Verification**: Problems with phone number verification
- **Email Verification**: Issues with email verification

### 5. Emotional and Psychological Indicators

#### Stress Indicators
- **Voice Stress**: Changes in voice tone, pitch, or speed
- **Typing Stress**: Changes in typing patterns under stress
- **Decision Making**: Indecisiveness or rapid decision changes
- **Memory Issues**: Difficulty remembering basic information
- **Concentration**: Difficulty concentrating or staying focused

#### Manipulation Indicators
- **Authority References**: Frequent references to authority figures
- **Urgency Claims**: Claims of urgent or emergency situations
- **Secrecy Requests**: Requests to keep information secret
- **Pressure Tactics**: Use of pressure or intimidation tactics
- **Emotional Appeals**: Excessive emotional appeals or manipulation

### 6. Social Engineering Indicators

#### Impersonation Attempts
- **Authority Claims**: Claims to be from legitimate organizations
- **Official Language**: Use of official-sounding language or terminology
- **Threats**: Threats of account closure or legal action
- **Rewards**: Promises of rewards or benefits
- **Urgency**: Claims of time-sensitive situations

#### Information Gathering
- **Personal Questions**: Excessive personal questions
- **Account Details**: Requests for account details or passwords
- **Verification Codes**: Requests for verification codes or tokens
- **Document Requests**: Requests for personal documents
- **Financial Information**: Requests for financial information

### 7. Risk Assessment Models

#### Behavioral Risk Scoring
- **Composite Scores**: Combined behavioral risk scores
- **Weighted Indicators**: Weighted importance of different indicators
- **Threshold Analysis**: Risk threshold analysis and alerting
- **Trend Analysis**: Analysis of behavioral trends over time
- **Comparative Analysis**: Comparison with peer groups or baselines

#### Machine Learning Models
- **Supervised Learning**: Models trained on known fraud cases
- **Unsupervised Learning**: Models for detecting unknown patterns
- **Ensemble Methods**: Combination of multiple models
- **Deep Learning**: Neural networks for complex pattern recognition
- **Graph Analysis**: Network-based behavioral analysis
